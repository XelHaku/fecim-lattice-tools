#### 2.2.1 The "Urban Mining" Initiative

A massive portion of Dr. Tour's recent work focuses on the recovery of critical minerals from waste, a strategic priority for national security and industrial supply chains. This context is vital because Dr. Jaeho Shin is listed as a co-author on several of these papers, indicating his versatility before focusing on IronLattice.

**Lithium Recovery (The research sample-ClO Process):**
Recent publications in *Science Advances* (October 2025) and Rice News highlight a breakthrough method for recovering lithium from spodumene ores and spent lithium-ion batteries. The "research sample-ClO" process utilizes Flash Joule Heating in the presence of chlorine gas to chemically transform lithium into extractable chlorides in seconds. This stands in stark contrast to traditional methods requiring months of acid leaching or evaporation ponds. The commercial entity scaling this is **Flash Metals USA**. Dr. Shin's co-authorship on these papers establishes his deep expertise in the research sample hardware, which he likely adapted for the IronLattice computing device.

**Rare Earth Elements (REEs):**
In parallel, the lab developed a method to recycle Rare Earth Elements from electronic waste, such as neodymium magnets. Published in *PNAS* (September 2025), this technique exploits the different vapor pressures of metals under flash heating to separate REEs with >90% purity. This project is also licensed to Flash Metals USA, aiming for production by early 2026.

**Red Mud Remediation:**
Addressing the aluminum industry's most toxic waste stream, the lab applied research sample to "red mud" (bauxite residue), recovering iron and converting the toxic sludge into safe ceramic materials. This work, published in *ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces*, further cements the lab's industrial relevance.

The investigation identified recent work on "Molecular Jackhammers" (2025-2026)—molecules activated by near-infrared light that mechanically disassemble cell membranes. While distinct from IronLattice, this highlights the multidisciplinary nature of the lab Dr. Shin emerged from.

### 3.3 The IronLattice Venture: Technical Deep Dive

Dr. Shin's importance in this network stems from his leadership of IronLattice, a spin-out described as developing a "superlattice-based ferroelectric device that enables analog, nonvolatile in-memory computation for artificial intelligence workloads".

**The Technological Thesis:**
Current AI computing is bottlenecked by the physical separation of memory (where data is stored) and processors (where data is computed). Moving data back and forth consumes massive amounts of energy—a phenomenon known as the "Von Neumann bottleneck." Dr. Shin's work focuses on In-Memory Computing (IMC), where the memory cell itself performs the calculation.

**The "Flash" Enabler:**
The competitive advantage of IronLattice likely lies in the manufacturing process. The research snippets highlight a paper titled "Stoichiometric Engineering of Indium Selenide Compounds Realized by Flash-within-Flash with an Arc Welder" (December 2025).

- **Material:** Indium Selenide (In₂Se₃) is a 2D ferroelectric semiconductor. "Ferroelectric" means it has a natural electrical polarization that can be switched—perfect for storing data (nonvolatile memory).
- **Process:** The "Flash-within-Flash" (FWF) method allows the Tour Group to synthesize these complex 2D materials in seconds using cheap precursors. This solves the primary hurdle of 2D materials: scalability.
- **Role of Dr. Shin:** He is listed as a primary contributor to the FWF and In₂Se₃ research. His transition from the lab to leading IronLattice suggests he has successfully packaged this synthesis method and device architecture into a commercially viable prototype.

**Professional Versatility:**
Dr. Shin is not limited to IronLattice. His name appears on papers regarding Lithium recovery, Rare Earths, and Red Mud. This indicates a high degree of technical competency across the entire research sample spectrum. However, his designation as the lead for IronLattice signals a specific career pivot toward the AI hardware market, making him the definitive point of contact for this specific vertical.
